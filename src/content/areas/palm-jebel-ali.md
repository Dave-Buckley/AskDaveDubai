Palm Jebel Ali is Nakheel's second palm island and, when complete, it will be the largest man-made island in the world. At 13.4 square kilometres, it is roughly twice the size of Palm Jumeirah. The masterplan includes 16 fronds (compared to Palm Jumeirah's 17), 91 kilometres of beachfront, and capacity for 80 hotels. The ambition is enormous - and so is the timeline.

The project was originally announced in the mid-2000s, paused after the 2008 financial crisis, and formally relaunched in 2023 with a radically expanded vision. Nakheel, a government-owned developer with a proven track record from Palm Jumeirah, is leading the project. That pedigree matters when you are buying into a development that will take years to fully deliver.

## What is Being Built

The masterplan is divided into five districts, each with a distinct purpose:

- **The Trunk** - the main connecting road from the mainland, designed for high-density apartments, retail, and commercial space
- **The Spine** - the central boulevard running through the island
- **The Fronds** - 16 gated neighbourhoods featuring ultra-luxury beachfront villas with private beach access
- **The Crown** - mixed-use hub with hotels, entertainment, and retail
- **The Crescents** - the outer arc, planned for world-class hotels and resorts including MGM Grand, Bellagio, and Aria

The villa collections on the fronds are the headline product. Five, six, and seven-bedroom villas with expansive living spaces, direct beach access, and the kind of exclusivity that Palm Jumeirah pioneered but can no longer replicate at scale.

## Pricing and How It Compares

Current off-plan pricing ranges from AED 2,500 to AED 2,800 per square foot, depending on villa collection and frond location. To put that in context:

- **Palm Jumeirah** currently trades at AED 3,000-6,000 per sqft for comparable beachfront product
- **Downtown Dubai** apartments sit at AED 2,200-4,000 per sqft - but of course Downtown offers urban living with immediate access to the Burj Khalifa, Dubai Mall, and established infrastructure

Five-bedroom villas have a median price of approximately AED 21.5 million. Six-bedroom villas sit at around AED 21.6 million. Premium seven-bedroom villas start from AED 43 million. These are not entry-level numbers, but the per-sqft value relative to Palm Jumeirah is significant.

Nakheel offers an 80/20 payment structure on most units, meaning 80% is paid during construction and 20% on handover. This keeps a meaningful portion of your capital available during the build period.

## Construction Progress

As of early 2026, island-wide construction completion sits at approximately 8.7%. Frond O is furthest along at around 22%, with Fronds I, J, M, and P also showing strong progress. AED 750 million of major infrastructure works are scheduled to complete by the end of 2026.

**Expected handover timeline:**
- **Late 2027** - first 325 units on Fronds I, J, M, and P
- **2028** - 746 units across Fronds A, C, D, E, F, and O
- **2029-2030** - remaining phases and hotel openings

More than 700 homes have been sold. Nakheel has awarded AED 5 billion (USD 1.36 billion) in construction contracts for villa works, which signals serious commitment to delivery.

## The Investment Case

The bull case is straightforward: you are buying beachfront property from a government-backed developer at 60-66% below the price of comparable product on Palm Jumeirah. As hotels open, amenities are delivered, and the community matures, capital values should appreciate substantially.

The bear case is equally clear: this is off-plan. Construction will take years. You are paying today for a community that does not yet exist. There is no established rental market, no schools, no retail, and no restaurants. If you need income from day one, this is the wrong purchase.

For comparison, Downtown Dubai offers immediate rental income (5-7% gross), walkable access to the Dubai Mall and Burj Khalifa, established schools and dining, and a liquid resale market. The trade-off is that Downtown will not give you a private beach, a villa with a garden, or the entry pricing that an under-construction mega-project offers.

Palm Jebel Ali suits patient capital. Investors and end-users who can wait until 2027-2030 for handover and who believe in the long-term thesis that Dubai's waterfront supply cannot keep pace with demand from global wealth migration.

## The Hotel Factor

The confirmed hotel brands are a significant anchor for future value. MGM Grand, Bellagio, and Aria - three of the most recognisable names in global hospitality - are planned for the Crescent. In total, 80 hotels with approximately 1,400 branded rooms are expected across the island.

For villa owners, the proximity of world-class hotels and their associated dining, spas, and beach clubs will define the lifestyle offering. It also underpins the holiday-home and short-term rental opportunity once the island is operational.

## Where to Eat

Palm Jebel Ali has no dining options on the island itself yet. This will change as hotels and the Trunk retail district come online, but for now, residents of the broader Jebel Ali area rely on nearby established options.

**JA The Resort (Jebel Ali Beach - 10 min drive)**
- Kinara by Vikas Khanna - Indian. Elevated North Indian cuisine in an elegant setting.
- White Orchid - Asian fusion. Relaxed resort dining.
- La Fontana - Mediterranean. Poolside brasserie.
- Anchor Bar - Casual. Beachside drinks and light bites.

**Ibn Battuta Mall (15 min drive)**
- Broad selection of casual dining chains and a large food court. The most convenient option for everyday meals until Palm Jebel Ali's own retail is built out.

**Dubai Marina / JBR (20 min drive)**
- Pier 7 - Multiple restaurants across seven floors overlooking the marina.
- The Maine Oyster Bar & Grill - Seafood and grill in a New England-inspired setting.
- Tresind Studio (DIFC, 30 min) - Indian fine dining. Michelin-starred.

*For destination dining, Downtown Dubai (35-40 min) offers At.mosphere in the Burj Khalifa, Zuma, and the full Dubai Mall restaurant collection.*

## Who Should Buy

**Good fit:**
- Investors with a 3-5 year horizon who want beachfront at below-market entry pricing
- End-users planning for a family villa and comfortable waiting for 2027-2030 handover
- Golden Visa seekers (properties well above the AED 2 million threshold)
- Buyers priced out of Palm Jumeirah who want a comparable product

**Not the right fit:**
- Anyone needing immediate rental income
- Buyers who want walkable urban living (Downtown Dubai or Business Bay are better options)
- Families who need established schools and community infrastructure now
- Risk-averse investors uncomfortable with off-plan construction timelines

Palm Jebel Ali is one of the most ambitious real estate projects anywhere in the world. The scale, the developer pedigree, and the pricing relative to Palm Jumeirah make a compelling case. But it requires patience, capital, and comfort with the reality that you are buying a vision that will take years to fully materialise.
