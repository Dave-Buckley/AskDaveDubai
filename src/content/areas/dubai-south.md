Dubai South is where you invest for the next decade, not the next year. When Al Maktoum International Airport becomes the world's busiest airport — a project the UAE government has committed significant capital to — demand for housing, logistics, and hospitality in the area will be extraordinary. The entry price today reflects the risk. And the opportunity.

The area came to global attention as the site of Expo 2020 Dubai, which attracted 24 million visitors over six months. The Expo site has since been repurposed as Expo City Dubai — a functioning mixed-use residential and commercial destination with tech companies, event venues, and residential accommodation.

**Emaar South** is the most credible residential community in the area, developed by Emaar and anchored by an 18-hole golf course. The brand recognition and Emaar's track record reduce the execution risk that comes with investing in an early-stage community.

**The airport thesis:** Al Maktoum International Airport's current expansion plan targets 260 million passengers annually when complete — more than twice the current capacity of Dubai International Airport. That level of aviation activity generates enormous downstream demand: aviation staff, ground crew, logistics workers, hospitality workers, and the supporting commercial ecosystem. All of them need housing.

**Metro connectivity is real today.** Route 2020, opened for Expo 2020, connects Dubai South to the Red Line at Jabal Ali station. This is a significant advantage over JVC and other car-dependent communities at similar price points.

For investors with patience: the yield today (7–9% gross on completed units) is attractive even before accounting for the catalysts. The entry price — Dubai's lowest for freehold — limits downside. The upside is as large as the airport.
