First impressions sell properties. A well-staged home photographs better, shows better, and ultimately sells faster and for more money. Here's how to stage your Dubai property without spending a fortune.

## Fix What's Broken First

Before you think about styling, address the basics. Cracks, creaky doors, leaking taps, and chipped paint all signal to buyers that the property hasn't been maintained. Get repairs done before anything else - it's the highest-return investment you can make in the selling process.

## Declutter Ruthlessly

A clean, tidy space looks bigger and more appealing. Remove personal items, excess furniture, and anything that makes rooms feel crowded. Buyers need to imagine themselves living there - and that's hard to do surrounded by someone else's clutter.

## Rearrange Furniture

Strategic furniture placement creates a welcoming, spacious feel:

- Don't position furniture with its back to the entrance
- Arrange pieces along walls to maintain open sightlines
- Create clear pathways through each room
- Remove any furniture that makes a room feel cramped

## Fresh Paint

A fresh coat of paint transforms a space for relatively little cost. Stick to neutral colours - whites, soft pastels, light blues, or warm greys. Avoid dark or bold colours that can make rooms feel smaller. Pay special attention to scuff marks, marks around light switches, and any walls that have faded.

## Maximise Natural Light

Natural light costs nothing and makes every room look better. Open all curtains and blinds before viewings. For rooms that don't get much natural light, supplement with well-placed lamps or subtle lighting.

## Stage the Key Rooms

Focus your effort on the rooms that matter most:

- **Living room** - Arrange seating to create a conversation area; add a few cushions and a throw
- **Bedrooms** - Make beds with fresh, crisp linens; keep nightstands minimal
- **Kitchen** - Clear countertops completely; display only a fruit bowl or a small plant
- **Bathrooms** - Fresh towels, clean grout, clear surfaces

## The Fine Details

Small touches make a disproportionate difference:

- Fresh flowers or a simple plant on the dining table
- Subtle scented candles (nothing overpowering)
- A few well-chosen photo frames or artwork
- Area rugs to define spaces in open-plan layouts
- A clean, tidy entrance - this is the first thing buyers see

## The Bottom Line

You don't need to spend thousands on staging. A deep clean, minor repairs, fresh paint, and thoughtful arrangement can be done on a minimal budget - and the return on that investment, in terms of sale price and time on market, is significant.
