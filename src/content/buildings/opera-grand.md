## Overview

Opera Grand is the premium residential tower in Downtown's Opera District, completed by Emaar in 2019. At 66 floors, it is taller than its neighbours Act One and Act Two, and it was designed to sit a tier above them in terms of finishes, layouts, and overall positioning. The clue is in the name - this building was conceived as the flagship residence of the Opera District, and it delivers on that promise.

The tower sits directly adjacent to Dubai Opera, giving many units unobstructed views of the iconic dhow-shaped performance venue. Upper floor units also get excellent Burj Khalifa and fountain views. The lobby and common areas are finished to a higher specification than typical Emaar projects, with quality materials and a more curated feel.

## Units and Layouts

With only 280 units across 66 floors, the density here is notably low. You are looking at roughly four to five units per floor, which translates to larger apartments with better privacy than most Downtown towers. The mix starts at one-bedroom and goes up to four-bedroom penthouses. There are no studios here - this was a deliberate decision to position the building at a higher tier.

The layouts are generous. Two-bedroom units offer genuine living space with well-proportioned rooms and en-suite bathrooms. Three and four-bedroom apartments are among the best-designed family units in Downtown, with separate living and dining areas, utility rooms, and maid's quarters in larger configurations. Finishes throughout are a step up from standard Emaar - expect upgraded kitchen appliances, better bathroom fixtures, and solid flooring.

## Who It Suits

Opera Grand is for buyers who want a premium Downtown residence without the branded service charges. If you want larger layouts, better finishes, and a quieter building than the towers around you, this delivers. It works equally well for families looking for three or four-bedroom living and for professionals who simply want a higher-quality home.

It is not the right choice if you are purely yield-driven. The higher price per square foot and service charges mean net yields will be lower than in Act One, Act Two, or Burj Royale down the road.
