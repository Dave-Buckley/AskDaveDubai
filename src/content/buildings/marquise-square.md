Marquise Square is a 56-floor tower by Deyaar, positioned on the southwestern edge of Downtown Dubai. It is one of the most affordable buildings in the district, and for investors chasing yield, it is consistently one of the strongest performers in the area.

## The Building

Completed in 2016, Marquise Square sits close to the boundary between Downtown and Business Bay. The building is well maintained and benefits from proximity to the Business Bay Metro station, which is a genuine advantage for tenants who commute. Dubai Mall and the Boulevard are within walking distance, though you are further from the fountain and Burj Khalifa than the core Downtown buildings.

The lobby and common areas are functional without being particularly luxurious. This is a building that prioritises practicality and value over prestige.

## Unit Layouts

One-bedroom apartments range from 750 to 900 square feet, with open-plan living areas and adequate kitchens. Two-bedroom units are between 1,100 and 1,400 square feet, offering good separation between bedrooms and a comfortable living space. Three-bedroom units are available in smaller numbers and suit families.

The fit-out is standard for the price point. Kitchens come with integrated appliances, bathrooms have decent fittings, and the floor-to-ceiling windows are a plus throughout.

## Service Charges

Service charges are among the lowest in Downtown at AED 14 to 17 per square foot per year. For a one-bedroom of 800 square feet, annual charges are roughly AED 11,000 to 13,500. That is a meaningful saving compared to most Downtown buildings and directly benefits net rental yields.

## Investment Profile

Marquise Square regularly delivers net yields of 7 to 8 percent, making it one of the highest-yielding buildings in the entire Downtown district. The combination of low purchase prices, low service charges, and strong rental demand creates an investment case that is hard to beat on pure numbers.

Tenants are primarily young professionals, couples, and single expats who want a Downtown postcode at a competitive rent. The Metro proximity is a genuine draw for this demographic.

## Who It Suits

Yield-focused investors. Budget-conscious tenants who want Downtown without the premium. Anyone who prioritises returns over a prestigious lobby.

## My Take

Marquise Square is the building I show clients when they say they want to invest in Downtown but are put off by the prices. The yields are strong, the running costs are low, and the rental demand is reliable. It is not the most glamorous address in the district, but when the numbers work, the numbers work. If your priority is return on investment rather than impressing dinner guests, Marquise Square deserves a serious look.
