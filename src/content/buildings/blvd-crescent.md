## Overview

BLVD Crescent is a different kind of Downtown living. Completed by Emaar in 2020, this low-rise development stands at just 25 floors - modest by Downtown standards where 40, 50, and 60-storey towers are the norm. With only around 200 units, it is one of the smaller residential communities in the district, and that exclusivity is by design. The building sits on the curved section of Mohammed Bin Rashid Boulevard, giving it the name and the frontage that comes with that address.

This is Emaar's answer to buyers who want the Downtown postcode but not the high-rise lifestyle. Fewer units means quieter corridors, shorter lift waits, and a sense of community that 500-unit towers simply cannot replicate. It is a different product for a different buyer, and the pricing reflects that.

## Units and Layouts

The unit mix here is broader than most Downtown buildings, running from one-bedroom all the way up to four-bedroom. One-beds start from around 800 square feet, two-beds from 1,300 square feet, three-beds from 1,800 square feet, and four-beds from 2,400 square feet. These are proper family-sized apartments with layouts that prioritise liveability over unit count.

Finishes are a clear step above standard Emaar. Being a 2020 completion with a premium positioning, the developer used higher-grade materials - better flooring, upgraded kitchen appliances, improved bathroom fittings, and more attention to detail in the common areas. Balconies are generous, and many units benefit from Boulevard views or partial Burj Khalifa sightlines.

The four-bedroom units are particularly noteworthy. Finding a four-bed apartment in Downtown that is not a penthouse or a serviced residence is rare, and BLVD Crescent fills that gap for families who need the space.

## Who It Suits

BLVD Crescent is built for families and professionals who want to live in Downtown without the constant buzz of tower life. If you have children and want a calmer environment with a Boulevard address, this is one of a handful of buildings that genuinely works. It also appeals to downsizers from villas who want apartment living but cannot stomach a 450 square foot studio on the 45th floor.

It is not the right building for yield-focused investors. The entry price is higher, the service charges are elevated, and the returns are modest compared to studio-heavy towers. If you are buying purely on numbers, look at Boulevard Central or 29 Boulevard instead.
