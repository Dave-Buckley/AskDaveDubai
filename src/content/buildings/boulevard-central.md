## Overview

Boulevard Central is one of Downtown Dubai's original residential offerings - a twin-tower Emaar complex completed in 2011 that sits right on Mohammed Bin Rashid Boulevard. Tower 1 and Tower 2 each stand at 32 floors, housing around 440 units between them. If you are looking for a way into Downtown without paying premium tower prices, this is one of the first buildings I point people towards.

The location is genuinely excellent. You are steps from the Boulevard, walking distance to Dubai Mall, and close to the Metro. For a building that is over a decade old, it holds its own because of that positioning. Downtown is all about location, and Boulevard Central nails it.

## Units and Layouts

The mix here is heavily weighted towards studios and one-bedrooms, with some two-bedroom units available. Studios range from around 400 to 500 square feet, one-beds from 650 to 850 square feet, and two-beds from 1,100 to 1,400 square feet. The layouts are straightforward - nothing fancy, but functional. Kitchens are mostly open-plan, and many units get decent Boulevard or partial Burj Khalifa views depending on the floor and orientation.

Being a 2011 build, the finishes are not going to compete with newer towers like Act One Act Two or The Address Residences. Flooring, cabinetry, and bathroom fittings show their age in some units. But plenty of owners have renovated, and a refreshed unit in this building represents solid value.

## Service Charges

Service charges run between AED 14 and 18 per square foot per year, which is on the lower end for Downtown. This is one of the real advantages of an older Emaar building - the community is established, the sinking fund is built up, and there are fewer of the teething issues you get with newer developments. For a studio owner, you are looking at roughly AED 6,000 to 9,000 per year in service charges, which is very manageable.

## Rental Market

Boulevard Central is a strong rental performer. Studios typically lease for AED 55,000 to 70,000 per year, and one-beds for AED 75,000 to 100,000 depending on the floor, view, and condition. The tenant profile is mostly young professionals and couples who want a Downtown address without the price tag of a newer building. Occupancy stays high because the location does the heavy lifting.

Holiday home rentals are worth exploring here too - studios in this location can perform well on short-term platforms given the proximity to Dubai Mall and Burj Khalifa.

## Who It Suits

This building works best for investors chasing yield and end-users who want a Downtown lifestyle on a tighter budget. If you are a first-time buyer looking at Downtown, Boulevard Central studios are among the most accessible entry points. It is also a solid pick for someone who wants to live on the Boulevard without spending AED 2M-plus.

It is less ideal if you want modern finishes, a gym that rivals a five-star hotel, or three-bedroom family living. The amenities are basic by current standards - a pool, gym, and not much else.

## My Take

Boulevard Central is the workhorse of Downtown. It is not glamorous, it is not going to win any design awards, and the lobbies feel dated compared to newer launches. But the fundamentals are rock solid - great location, reasonable service charges, and consistent rental demand. I have seen too many buyers chase shiny new towers with inflated service charges and questionable build quality. Boulevard Central will not excite you on a viewing, but it will deliver steady returns and a genuine Downtown lifestyle. For investors focused on the numbers rather than the brochure, this one makes sense.
