## Overview

Vida Residences Downtown is the bigger sibling to Vida Downtown. Completed in 2019, this 56-storey tower sits on Mohammed Bin Rashid Boulevard, right in the heart of Downtown Dubai. At 280 units, it is significantly larger than the original Vida (175 units) and offers a wider range of unit types including three-bedroom apartments.

The building brings the Vida lifestyle approach - design-forward, younger demographic, co-working spaces - to a more prominent Boulevard location. It is closer to the action than Vida Downtown, with direct Boulevard frontage and easier access to Dubai Mall and the Fountain.

One notable feature is the co-working lounge in the lobby. It reflects the Vida brand's understanding that many residents and guests work remotely and want a professional space outside their apartment. It is a small detail but a useful one.

## Units and Layouts

The range runs from studios through to three-bedroom apartments, giving this building a broader appeal than its smaller sibling. Studios are compact at around 400-500 square feet, designed for efficiency rather than space. One-bedroom units (700-900 square feet) are the most popular with investors. Two and three-bedroom units cater to families and those wanting more living space.

Interiors follow the Vida aesthetic - contemporary, clean, and lighter in tone than Address properties. The finishes are good quality without being overly luxurious. It feels modern and fresh, which photographs well for rental listings.

Boulevard-facing units benefit from the street-level energy below and partial views toward the Burj Khalifa. Units on higher floors with Burj Khalifa views command a noticeable premium.

## Service Charges

Service charges sit at AED 30-38 per square foot per year. That is more reasonable than Vida Downtown (which runs higher due to its smaller unit count) and roughly in line with the Address Boulevard building. The larger number of units here helps distribute operating costs more evenly.

You are still paying for hotel-branded services - concierge, pool and gym, building maintenance, common area upkeep, and the Vida experience. This is not a standard residential service charge, so do not compare it directly to non-branded towers.

## Rental Market

This building is popular with short-term rental investors, and for good reason. The Boulevard location, Vida branding, and modern interiors make it an easy listing on holiday home platforms. Studios and one-bedroom units are the workhorses of the short-term rental market here.

Peak season (November to March) drives the strongest returns, with tourists and business travellers looking for well-located, lifestyle-oriented accommodation. Off-peak months are quieter and you need to price competitively to maintain occupancy.

Long-term tenants tend to be young professionals and remote workers who appreciate the co-working spaces, the modern design, and the Boulevard location. It is a good building for attracting tenants who want more personality than a generic residential tower.

You are on the Boulevard alongside Address Boulevard and several other branded and non-branded towers, all targeting similar tenants and guests.

## Resale Market

Resale activity is healthy. The lower average price per square foot compared to Address properties makes this one of the more accessible branded residences in Downtown Dubai. Entry prices for studios can sit below AED 900K, which attracts first-time investors and those building a portfolio of smaller units.

The Vida brand is still establishing its international reputation, so you may find that some overseas buyers are less familiar with it than with Address. Locally, however, it is well-recognised and the building sells on its own merits.

## Who It Suits

Vida Residences Downtown suits investors looking for hotel-branded returns at a more accessible price point than Address. It works well for buy-to-let strategies, particularly studios and one-beds targeting the short-term rental market. End-users who want Boulevard living with lifestyle amenities and a younger vibe will also feel at home here.

## My Take

Vida Residences Downtown is one of the better value propositions in Downtown Dubai's branded residence market. You get Vida hotel services, a Boulevard address, and modern interiors at a lower per-square-foot cost than the Address equivalents. The co-working spaces are a genuinely useful addition that reflects how people actually live and work in 2026.

The honest reality is that Vida does not carry the same prestige as Address. If you are hosting guests who specifically search for "Address Hotel Dubai" on booking platforms, they will not find your Vida listing in that search. Vida has its own audience, and that audience is growing, but it is not interchangeable with Address.

For the numbers, this building can make sense. The entry price is lower, the service charges are more manageable than Vida Downtown, and the Boulevard location is strong. Studios here are genuine workhorse investments if you manage them well and price them realistically.

If you want branded residence exposure in Downtown without stretching to Address pricing, this is where I would point you. Just be clear on the difference between Vida and Address positioning, and make sure your rental strategy aligns with the guest demographic this building attracts.
