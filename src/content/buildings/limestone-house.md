Limestone House is part of Downtown Dubai's Old Town district, a collection of low-rise buildings that offer a completely different feel to the glass towers that dominate the rest of the area. If you want to live in Downtown but the idea of a 60-floor high-rise does not appeal, Old Town is your answer, and Limestone House is one of its best addresses.

## The Building

At just six floors, Limestone House is the antithesis of the typical Downtown tower. The architecture draws on traditional Arabian design with sandstone facades, arched windows, and shaded walkways. It feels more like a Mediterranean village than a Dubai development, which is exactly the point.

The building sits in the Old Town cluster, steps away from Souk Al Bahar and the Burj Khalifa lake. You can walk to the fountain shows, the Palace Downtown hotel, and Dubai Mall within minutes, but your immediate surroundings are quiet, tree-lined, and residential. It is one of the most pleasant places to walk in the entire district.

## Unit Layouts

Units range from one to three bedrooms. The layouts in Old Town buildings tend to be generous compared to the towers, with larger living areas and more storage space. Many units feature balconies or terraces overlooking the courtyards and landscaped pathways that connect the Old Town buildings.

The fit-out reflects the older vintage, but many owners have updated their apartments. The bones of the building are solid, and the original Emaar quality shows.

## Service Charges

Service charges sit in the AED 16 to 20 per square foot range. For a two-bedroom of around 1,500 square feet, annual charges come in at AED 24,000 to 30,000. That is reasonable for the location and the Emaar management standard.

## Investment Profile

Old Town properties, including Limestone House, attract a specific buyer and tenant profile. Families, couples, and professionals who value the quiet, village-like atmosphere over high-floor views and branded amenities. The rental market is steady, with tenants who tend to stay longer than in the towers.

Yields are moderate at 5 to 6 percent, but the tenant retention is better than average, which reduces void periods and management hassle.

## My Take

Limestone House and the broader Old Town district are for people who want Downtown without the glass-and-steel intensity. If you have walked through Old Town on a cool evening, past the lit-up Souk Al Bahar with the Burj Khalifa behind it, you understand the appeal immediately. It is one of the most characterful neighbourhoods in all of Dubai, and Limestone House is a solid option within it. I recommend it to clients who tell me they want Downtown but they do not want to feel like they live in a hotel corridor.
